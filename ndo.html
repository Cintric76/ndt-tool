NDT Techniques (Overview)

Select a technique from the list below to view its description, advantages, limitations, and practical constraints.

Visual Testing (VT)

Direct or remote visual examination of surfaces.

Advantages

  • Lowest cost
  • Fast coverage
  • In-service capable

Limitations

  • Surface-visible only
  • Access/lighting dependent
  • Subjective

Temperature

Typical: -10 to +50 ¬∞C (stand-off optics up to ~200 ¬∞C)

Material / limitations

All materials; needs line-of-sight and clean surface

Liquid Penetrant (PT)

Dye penetrant reveals surface-breaking discontinuities.

Advantages

  • Very sensitive to tight cracks
  • Low cost
  • Works on non-magnetic materials

Limitations

  • Surface-breaking only
  • Needs thorough cleaning
  • Not for porous materials

Temperature

Standard: +5 to +50 ¬∞C (high-temp systems up to ~200 ¬∞C)

Material / limitations

Non-porous surfaces; metals and some non-metals

Magnetic Particle (MT)

Magnetization + particles indicate surface/near-surface defects.

Advantages

  • Excellent for cracking
  • Fast results
  • Relatively low cost

Limitations

  • Ferromagnetic only
  • Limited depth (few mm)
  • May require demagnetization

Temperature

Wet method: -10 to +50 ¬∞C (dry powder up to ~300 ¬∞C)

Material / limitations

Ferromagnetic steels only (not Al/Cu/austenitic SS)

Ultrasonic Testing (UT)

Pulse-echo ultrasound for flaws and thickness measurement.

Advantages

  • Internal defects + thickness
  • One-side access possible
  • No radiation

Limitations

  • Needs coupling/surface condition
  • Orientation sensitive
  • Operator dependent

Temperature

Standard: -10 to +50 ¬∞C (high-temp probes/couplants ~250‚Äì300 ¬∞C)

Material / limitations

Best on homogeneous metals; limited on coarse-grain/cast/austenitic welds

Radiography (RT ‚Äì Film)

X-ray/gamma transmission recorded on film.

Advantages

  • Permanent record
  • Good for volumetric flaws
  • Strong weld imaging (porosity/slag)

Limitations

  • Radiation controls
  • Cracks weak unless oriented
  • Often needs access both sides

Temperature

Film handling typically <~50 ¬∞C (part temp usually not limiting)

Material / limitations

Most materials; limited by thickness/density and access

Radiography (RT ‚Äì Digital)

Transmission radiography using CR plates or DR detectors.

Advantages

  • Faster than film
  • Digital storage/enhancement
  • Dose optimization possible

Limitations

  • High CAPEX
  • Detector fragility
  • Cracks still challenging

Temperature

Detectors/plates typically 0 to +40 ¬∞C (OEM dependent)

Material / limitations

Same as film RT; thickness/density constraints apply

Eddy Current (ET)

Induced currents in conductive materials detect surface flaws.

Advantages

  • No couplant
  • Fast
  • Can work through thin coatings

Limitations

  • Conductive only
  • Limited depth
  • Lift-off/geometry sensitive

Temperature

Typical: -10 to +60 ¬∞C (high-temp probes ~200 ¬∞C)

Material / limitations

Electrically conductive materials only

Phased Array UT (PAUT)

Multi-element UT with electronic beam steering and focusing.

Advantages

  • High POD
  • Multi-angle coverage
  • Digital imaging and reporting

Limitations

  • Complex setup
  • Higher cost
  • Advanced training required

Temperature

Typical: -10 to +50 ¬∞C (high-temp setups ~250‚Äì300 ¬∞C)

Material / limitations

Metals with acceptable attenuation; challenges in coarse-grain/austenitic welds

TOFD

Uses diffracted waves from crack tips for accurate sizing.

Advantages

  • Excellent height sizing
  • Good for planar flaws
  • Fast encoded weld scanning

Limitations

  • Near-surface dead zones
  • Needs good coupling/access
  • Often paired with PAUT

Temperature

Typical: -10 to +50 ¬∞C (high-temp ~250 ¬∞C)

Material / limitations

Best on welds/plate/pipe metals; poor on highly scattering materials

Guided Wave UT (GWUT)

Long-range ultrasonic screening along pipes/tubes.

Advantages

  • Screens long lengths
  • CUI screening
  • Minimal insulation removal

Limitations

  • Screening only
  • Reflections from supports
  • Follow-up local NDT required

Temperature

Typical: -20 to +80 ¬∞C (high-temp collars up to ~200 ¬∞C)

Material / limitations

Primarily metallic pipes/tubes with relatively uniform cross-section

Full Matrix Capture (FMC)

Records all TX/RX element combinations for advanced imaging.

Advantages

  • Data rich
  • Enables TFM
  • Improved characterization in difficult cases

Limitations

  • Slower acquisition
  • More processing
  • Higher cost/complexity

Temperature

Similar to PAUT hardware: -10 to +50 ¬∞C (high-temp with special probes)

Material / limitations

Metals; challenged by high attenuation/scatter

Total Focusing Method (TFM)

Post-processing of FMC data for high-resolution focused images.

Advantages

  • Excellent resolution
  • Strong characterization
  • Good for complex welds

Limitations

  • Computational load
  • Needs good data quality
  • Skill intensive

Temperature

As FMC/PAUT: typical -10 to +50 ¬∞C (high-temp setups available)

Material / limitations

As FMC; best on metals with manageable attenuation

Creeping Wave UT

Near-surface UT mode for defects near weld cap/root/HAZ.

Advantages

  • Improves near-surface detection
  • Useful on certain weld geometries

Limitations

  • Setup sensitive
  • Interpretation complex
  • Limited application envelope

Temperature

As UT: typical -10 to +50 ¬∞C (high-temp possible with special gear)

Material / limitations

Best on steel welds; surface condition influences performance

High-Temp UT (HT-UT)

Ultrasonic inspection performed on hot components.

Advantages

  • Avoids shutdown
  • In-service thickness monitoring

Limitations

  • Couplant/probe wear
  • Safety/access challenges
  • Lower stability

Temperature

Commonly ~150‚Äì300 ¬∞C (special systems higher, application-specific)

Material / limitations

Metallic components; heavy scale/oxidation reduces coupling

EMAT

Couplant-free ultrasound using electromagnetic generation.

Advantages

  • No couplant
  • Works on hot/rough surfaces
  • Repeatable scanning

Limitations

  • Lower signal amplitude
  • Lift-off sensitive
  • More specialized

Temperature

Often up to ~500 ¬∞C (system dependent)

Material / limitations

Electrically conductive materials; performance varies with magnetic properties

Computed Radiography (CR)

Radiography using imaging plates scanned into digital images.

Advantages

  • Reusable plates
  • Digital workflow
  • More flexible than DR

Limitations

  • Plate handling/scan time
  • Plate wear
  • Still radiation-controlled

Temperature

Plates/scanners typically 0 to +40 ¬∞C

Material / limitations

Same as RT; thickness/density constraints

Digital Radiography (DR)

Radiography using flat panel detectors (near real-time imaging).

Advantages

  • Very fast results
  • Immediate feedback
  • Digital dose optimization

Limitations

  • Expensive
  • Detector damage risk
  • Still limited for planar cracks

Temperature

Detectors typically 0 to +40 ¬∞C (OEM dependent)

Material / limitations

Same as RT; thickness/density constraints

Computed Tomography (CT)

3D radiographic reconstruction for internal geometry/defects.

Advantages

  • True 3D imaging
  • Excellent characterization
  • Accurate sizing (within limits)

Limitations

  • Very expensive
  • Slow
  • Part size/thickness limits

Temperature

Typically lab/room-temperature process (system dependent)

Material / limitations

Best for small/medium parts; dense/thick metals are challenging

Pulsed Eddy Current (PEC)

Low-frequency pulsed EM for corrosion/thickness through insulation.

Advantages

  • CUI screening
  • Minimal surface prep
  • Good area coverage

Limitations

  • Low spatial resolution
  • Limited sizing
  • Geometry/support effects

Temperature

Commonly up to ~200 ¬∞C (some systems up to ~500 ¬∞C)

Material / limitations

Conductive materials (often carbon steel); insulation type/thickness affects results

Remote Field Testing (RFT)

EM tube inspection method mainly for ferromagnetic tubing.

Advantages

  • Good for tube wall loss
  • Less sensitive to deposits
  • Used in heat exchangers

Limitations

  • Slow
  • Limited crack detection
  • Tube-focused method

Temperature

Typically 0 to +60 ¬∞C (probe electronics)

Material / limitations

Best for ferromagnetic (carbon steel) tubes

ACFM

EM method for surface-breaking cracks (often through coatings).

Advantages

  • Works through coatings
  • Good crack length sizing
  • Less prep than PT/MT

Limitations

  • Surface-breaking only
  • Geometry effects
  • Skilled use required

Temperature

Typical: -10 to +60 ¬∞C (high-temp probes exist)

Material / limitations

Conductive materials; widely used on steel structures (coated/uncoated)

Magnetic Flux Leakage (MFL)

Magnetization + sensors detect metal loss (tanks/pipes).

Advantages

  • Fast scanning
  • Good for corrosion/metal loss
  • Mature method

Limitations

  • Weak for tight cracks
  • Calibration needed for sizing
  • Ferromagnetic only

Temperature

Typical: -10 to +60 ¬∞C (special systems up to ~200 ¬∞C)

Material / limitations

Ferromagnetic materials only (carbon steel tanks/pipes)

SLOFEC

Saturation + low-frequency eddy currents for corrosion under coatings/scale.

Advantages

  • Better penetration than standard ET
  • Optimized for ferromagnetic steel

Limitations

  • Complex
  • Geometry sensitive
  • Less common availability

Temperature

Typically 0 to +60 ¬∞C (variants may allow higher)

Material / limitations

Conductive; often optimized for ferromagnetic steels

Thermography (IRT)

IR imaging to detect thermal anomalies (passive/active).

Advantages

  • Non-contact
  • Fast area coverage
  • In-service capable

Limitations

  • Needs thermal contrast
  • Limited depth
  • Emissivity/reflections affect results

Temperature

Camera dependent: commonly -20 to +350 ¬∞C (higher with specialty)

Material / limitations

All materials; surface emissivity management needed

Laser Shearography

Laser interferometry maps strain under load to reveal subsurface flaws.

Advantages

  • Full-field
  • Excellent for composites/bonds
  • Non-contact

Limitations

  • Vibration sensitive
  • Controlled loading needed
  • Less for thick metals

Temperature

Typically 0 to +40 ¬∞C (system/environment constraints)

Material / limitations

Best for composites/honeycomb/bonds; requires optical access

Laser Ultrasonics (LU)

Laser-generated ultrasound with optical detection (non-contact UT).

Advantages

  • No couplant
  • Works on hot/moving parts
  • Automation friendly

Limitations

  • Expensive
  • Alignment/surface reflectivity sensitive
  • Laser safety controls

Temperature

Stand-off possible on hot parts (hundreds ¬∞C), system dependent

Material / limitations

Many solids with optical access; surface condition affects performance

Acoustic Emission (AE)

Sensors detect transient waves from active damage during loading.

Advantages

  • Global monitoring
  • In-service possible
  • Good for proof/pressure tests

Limitations

  • Does not size defects directly
  • Noise discrimination needed
  • Needs loading

Temperature

Sensors typically -20 to +80 ¬∞C (high-temp up to ~500 ¬∞C, waveguides possible)

Material / limitations

Many materials; coupling/attenuation and sensor mounting quality matter

Digital Image Correlation (DIC)

Optical full-field strain/displacement from speckle tracking.

Advantages

  • Full-field strain
  • Non-contact
  • Great for validation and crack opening studies

Limitations

  • Needs line-of-sight
  • Lighting sensitive
  • Not an internal defect method

Temperature

Typically 0 to +40 ¬∞C (high-temp variants exist)

Material / limitations

Any visible surface with speckle/texture

3D Optical Scanning

Structured light scanning for 3D geometry and profiling.

Advantages

  • Fast geometry capture
  • Great for deformation/corrosion profiling
  • Digital records

Limitations

  • Line-of-sight only
  • Shiny surfaces can be difficult
  • No internal defects

Temperature

Typically 0 to +40 ¬∞C (optics constraints)

Material / limitations

Any surface; shiny/transparent may need matting

AI-assisted VT

Machine vision for surface detection, grading, and trending.

Advantages

  • Repeatable screening
  • Fast
  • Scales well

Limitations

  • Needs training data
  • False calls possible
  • Still needs human validation

Temperature

Typically 0 to +50 ¬∞C (stand-off possible for hotter items)

Material / limitations

Any visible surface; consistent imaging conditions required

Microwave NDT

Microwave interaction for moisture/dielectric changes and anomalies.

Advantages

  • Good for moisture/voids in dielectrics
  • Non-ionizing

Limitations

  • Limited in conductive materials
  • Application-specific calibration

Temperature

Typically 0 to +50 ¬∞C (electronics)

Material / limitations

Best for non-conductive materials (composites, plastics, insulation)

Ground Penetrating Radar (GPR)

EM pulses locate interfaces/objects in concrete/soil/asphalt.

Advantages

  • Fast scanning
  • Rebar/duct/void detection
  • Non-ionizing

Limitations

  • Poor in wet/conductive media
  • Interpretation intensive
  • Depth-resolution tradeoff

Temperature

Typically -10 to +50 ¬∞C (field electronics)

Material / limitations

Best for concrete/asphalt/soil; limited in salty/wet conductive conditions

Structural Health Monitoring (SHM)

Permanent sensors for condition trending (strain/vibration/AE/UT).

Advantages

  • Continuous monitoring
  • Detects change over time
  • Reduces manual inspections

Limitations

  • Needs baseline + data management
  • Sensor durability issues
  • Thresholds can be tricky

Temperature

Sensor dependent (often -40 to +85 ¬∞C; higher with special sensors)

Material / limitations

Depends on sensor type and mounting; broad applicability

UT Corrosion Mapping

Encoded or grid UT thickness mapping for wall loss distribution.

Advantages

  • Quantitative thickness map
  • Good for remaining life inputs
  • Automatable

Limitations

  • Needs surface access/prep
  • Slow without automation
  • Coupling affected by coatings/roughness

Temperature

As UT: typical -10 to +50 ¬∞C (high-temp options exist)

Material / limitations

Metals; limited on attenuative castings/composites

Online UT Monitoring

Permanently mounted UT sensors for corrosion/erosion trending.

Advantages

  • Trending at key points
  • Fewer shutdowns
  • Good for high-risk circuits

Limitations

  • Point measurement only
  • Installation quality critical
  • Long-term coupling challenges

Temperature

System dependent (often up to ~150‚Äì250 ¬∞C; higher with waveguides)

Material / limitations

Metals; requires reliable permanent mounting surface

Electrochemical Probes

Indirect corrosion monitoring (LPR/ER) for corrosion rate.

Advantages

  • Real-time corrosion rate
  • Useful for process control
  • Complements thickness data

Limitations

  • Indirect (not defect detection)
  • Chemistry dependent
  • Probe maintenance required

Temperature

Probe dependent (often ambient to ~200 ¬∞C)

Material / limitations

Requires installable probe locations in representative process environments

Neutron Radiography

Neutron imaging with unique contrast for light elements inside metal.

Advantages

  • Excellent for water/hydrogen in metal
  • Unique contrast vs X-ray

Limitations

  • Specialized facilities
  • High cost
  • Limited availability

Temperature

Typically facility/lab constraints

Material / limitations

Use-case driven (hydrogen/water content); facility constraints dominate

X-ray Backscatter

Single-side radiographic imaging using scattered X-rays.

Advantages

  • One-side access
  • Useful in some field constraints

Limitations

  • Lower penetration/resolution vs transmission RT
  • Still radiation
  • Application-specific

Temperature

Detector/electronics typically 0 to +40 ¬∞C

Material / limitations

Thickness/density limited; not ideal for very thick steel

Terahertz (THz)

THz waves for coatings, composites, moisture and layer defects.

Advantages

  • Non-ionizing
  • Good for coatings/layers
  • Can detect delamination/moisture

Limitations

  • Poor in metals
  • Water absorption effects
  • Specialized/expensive

Temperature

Typically 0 to +40 ¬∞C (instrument)

Material / limitations

Best for dielectrics (polymers/composites/coatings); not for bulk metals

Resonance Testing

Mechanical resonance response for screening stiffness/defect changes.

Advantages

  • Fast screening
  • Good for production sorting
  • Detects gross anomalies

Limitations

  • Not localizing
  • Needs baseline
  • Geometry sensitive

Temperature

Typically 0 to +50 ¬∞C (instrument); part may be warmer if fixtured

Material / limitations

Many solids; requires consistent geometry and boundary conditions

Vibration Analysis

Condition monitoring of rotating equipment via vibration trending.

Advantages

  • Excellent for rotating assets
  • Early warning via trends
  • Non-intrusive

Limitations

  • Not a direct material flaw method
  • Operating condition dependent
  • Expertise required

Temperature

Sensor dependent (often -40 to +120 ¬∞C; higher with specialty sensors)

Material / limitations

Machines/structures (not material-specific)
